Managing Seasonal Allergies Naturally

📅 May 15, 2026 | 🏷️ Allergies

Seasonal allergies affect millions of Kenyans, especially during the dry and dusty months. From sneezing fits to itchy eyes, allergies can make daily life miserable. Here are natural ways to find relief.

1. Identify Your Triggers

Common allergy triggers in Kenya include:
• Dust mites — found in bedding, carpets, and upholstered furniture
• Pollen — from grass, trees, and flowering plants
• Mold — thrives in damp areas during the rainy season
• Pet dander — from cats, dogs, and other furry animals
Keep a symptom diary to identify patterns and avoid your specific triggers.

2. Natural Remedies That Work

Saline nasal rinse: A neti pot or squeeze bottle with sterile saline solution flushes out allergens from your nasal passages. Use daily during allergy season.
Local honey: Some studies suggest a teaspoon of locally sourced honey daily may help desensitize your body to local pollen. While not a cure, many Kenyans swear by it.
Quercetin-rich foods: Found in apples, onions, green tea, and berries — quercetin is a natural antihistamine that can reduce allergy symptoms.
Steam inhalation: Add a few drops of eucalyptus or peppermint oil to hot water and inhale steam to clear blocked sinuses.
Vitamin C: A natural antihistamine and immune booster. Increase your intake of oranges, mangoes, kales, and bell peppers.

3. Home Environment Adjustments

• Keep windows closed during high-pollen hours (early morning and late afternoon)
• Use a HEPA air purifier in your bedroom
• Wash bedding weekly in hot water (above 60°C) to kill dust mites
• Remove shoes at the door to avoid tracking in pollen
• Dust surfaces with a damp cloth instead of a dry duster
• Keep indoor humidity below 50% to discourage mold growth

4. When to See a Pharmacist

If natural remedies aren't enough, talk to our pharmacists about:
• Antihistamines: Non-drowsy options like cetirizine or loratadine
• Nasal corticosteroids: Sprays that reduce inflammation
• Eye drops: For itchy, watery eyes
• Allergy testing: To identify specific triggers

Important: Severe allergic reactions involving difficulty breathing, swelling of the face or throat, or rapid heartbeat require immediate medical attention.
