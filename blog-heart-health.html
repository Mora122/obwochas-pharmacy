Heart Health: Simple Lifestyle Changes That Save Lives

📅 May 3, 2026 | 🏷️ Heart Health

Cardiovascular disease is one of Kenya's leading causes of death, yet many cases are preventable through simple lifestyle changes. Your heart works tirelessly — here's how to take care of it.

1. Know Your Numbers

Understanding your heart health starts with knowing these key numbers:
• Blood pressure: Healthy is below 120/80 mmHg. High blood pressure often has no symptoms but damages arteries over time.
• Cholesterol: Total cholesterol should be below 5.2 mmol/L. LDL ("bad") cholesterol should be below 3.0 mmol/L.
• Blood sugar: Fasting blood glucose should be below 6.1 mmol/L.
• BMI: A body mass index below 25 is considered healthy for most adults.

2. Eat for Your Heart

A heart-healthy Kenyan diet includes:
• More fruits and vegetables: Aim for at least 5 portions daily. Include sukuma wiki, spinach, mangoes, oranges, and avocados
• Whole grains: Replace white maize meal with whole-grain options like millet, sorghum, or brown rice
• Healthy fats: Avocado, nuts, seeds, and olive oil. Limit fried foods and fatty meats
• Reduce salt: Kenyans consume far too much salt. Aim for less than 5g (one teaspoon) daily. Use herbs and spices instead
• Limit sugar: Cut back on soda, sweetened chai, and processed snacks

3. Get Moving

The World Health Organization recommends:
• At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week (30 minutes, 5 days a week)
• Activities like brisk walking, cycling, swimming, or dancing
• Include strength training twice a week for additional benefits
• Start slow if you've been inactive — even a 10-minute walk is better than nothing

4. Manage Stress

Chronic stress raises blood pressure and increases heart disease risk. Try these stress-busters:
• Practice deep breathing (4-7-8 technique: inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 7, exhale for 8)
• Get 7-8 hours of quality sleep each night
• Stay socially connected with family and friends
• Take breaks from work and screens
• Consider meditation or prayer for mental calm

5. Warning Signs to Watch For

Seek medical help immediately if you experience:
• Chest discomfort (pressure, squeezing, or pain)
• Shortness of breath
• Pain or numbness in your arms, back, neck, jaw, or stomach
• Cold sweat, nausea, or lightheadedness
• Irregular heartbeat or palpitations
